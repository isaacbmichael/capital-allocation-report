Conclusions & Recommendations

Rebalancing frequency affects both downside control and upside capture. In these simulations:

  • Fixed: Higher drift; tends to amplify both tails.
  • Annual vs Semi-Annual: Semi-Annual often tightens downside slightly; Annual can leave a bit more room for upside to run.
  • Quarterly/Monthly: Most risk-controlled; may trim some extreme upside.

For executives balancing risk and innovation exposure, a rebalanced Crypto/AI mix (Annual or Semi-Annual) often delivers strong median outcomes with disciplined downside.

Appendix A – Methodology

This report simulates 20-year investment outcomes using a bootstrap Monte Carlo approach based on daily historical returns from 2015-01-01 to 2025-01-01. We compare Fixed (no rebalancing) and Rebalanced strategies at multiple frequencies:

  • Annual: Reset weights once per year.
  • Semi-Annual: Reset weights twice per year (every ~6 months).
  • Quarterly: Reset weights four times per year.
  • Monthly: Reset weights 12 times per year.

CFO Policy: Conditional allocation to BTC/AI if benchmark return exceeds 8% annually.

Appendix C – Strategy Definitions

  • 100% BTC: Entire portfolio in Bitcoin.
  • 100% XRP: Entire portfolio in Ripple (XRP).
  • AI Blend: Equal-weighted NVDA, MSFT, GOOGL, AAPL, TSLA, META, AMZN, AMD.
  • Crypto Blend: Equal-weighted BTC, ETH, XRP, SOL.
  • 60% Crypto / 40% AI: 60% Crypto Blend + 40% AI Blend.
  • 70% Crypto / 30% AI: 70% Crypto Blend + 30% AI Blend.
  • 40% AI / 60% Benchmark: Balanced allocation with benchmark.
  • 70% AI / 30% Crypto: 70% AI Blend + 30% Crypto Blend.
  • CFO Policy (Target 8%): Allocate to BTC/AI only if benchmark > 8% annual return.
