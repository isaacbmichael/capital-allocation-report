Conclusions & Recommendations

These simulations highlight clear differences between benchmark exposure, concentrated innovation bets, and dynamic allocation rules:

  • VOO (Benchmark): Provides the most stable compounding (≈8% CAGR) with very low probability of long-term loss. A reliable core allocation.
  • AI or Crypto Blends: Exhibit extreme upside potential but poor median outcomes and high loss probabilities. These behave like “lottery ticket” strategies.
  • Rebalancing: Annual or Semi-Annual rebalancing strikes a better balance than Quarterly or Monthly, which tend to dampen upside more than they reduce risk.
  • CFO Policy (Target 8%): Clearly outperforms on a risk-adjusted basis — delivering the highest median CAGR (~10%), nearly 40% chance of millionaire outcomes, and only moderate loss risk (~7%).

Recommendation: For executives balancing growth with discipline, the CFO Policy allocation stands out as the most attractive strategy. A diversified benchmark core with conditional exposure to high-growth assets provides both resilience and meaningful upside.

Appendix A – Methodology

This report simulates 20-year investment outcomes using a multivariate moving-block bootstrap on business-day returns from 2015-01-01 to 2025-01-01. Blocks of length 21 are resampled across all assets simultaneously to preserve cross-asset correlation and volatility clustering.

To avoid overstating expected growth, we remove the in-sample mean (“drift leakage”) and then apply forward-looking annual drifts together with per-asset fees. This yields more realistic median growth while retaining fat-tailed distributions.

  • Fixed (Buy & Hold): Portfolio weights drift naturally; no scheduled rebalancing.
  • Rebalanced: True rebalancing at the stated frequency (Annual, Semi-Annual, Quarterly, Monthly), with transaction costs of 10 bps applied on traded notional, and tolerance bands of 0%.
  • CFO Policy: If the benchmark’s simulated annual return exceeds 8%, the portfolio shifts to a 50/50 BTC–AI blend for the next year; otherwise it remains in the benchmark. Switching incurs transaction costs.

Note: Forward-looking drifts, fees, and rebalancing rules are conservative defaults and can be modified at the top of the notebook. This methodology produces credible long-run medians and realistic short-run dynamics consistent with observed market behavior.

Appendix C – Strategy Definitions

  • 100% BTC: Entire portfolio allocated to Bitcoin (BTC-USD).
  • 100% XRP: Entire portfolio allocated to Ripple (XRP-USD).
  • AI Blend: Equal-weighted basket of major AI/tech equities (NVDA, MSFT, GOOGL, AAPL, TSLA, META, AMZN, AMD).
  • Crypto Blend: Equal-weighted basket of major cryptocurrencies (BTC-USD, ETH-USD, XRP-USD, SOL-USD).
  • 60% Crypto / 40% AI: 60% Crypto Blend + 40% AI Blend.
  • 70% Crypto / 30% AI: 70% Crypto Blend + 30% AI Blend.
  • 40% AI / 60% VOO: 40% AI Blend + 60% VOO (benchmark ETF or index).
  • 70% AI / 30% Crypto: 70% AI Blend + 30% Crypto Blend.
  • 100% VOO: Entire portfolio allocated to the benchmark (VOO).
  • CFO Policy (Target 8%): Each year, if the benchmark return exceeds 8%, the portfolio shifts to a 50/50 BTC–AI Blend mix; otherwise it remains in the benchmark. Switching incurs transaction costs.
